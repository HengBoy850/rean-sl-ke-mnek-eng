  "The General Shift Cipher (also called the Shift Cipher) is a classical substitution cipher in which each letter of the plaintext is shifted by a fixed number of positions in the alphabet. The Caesar Cipher is simply a special case of the general shift cipher with a shift of 3."

I.III. Affine Cipher

  "The Affine Cipher is a classical substitution cipher that improves upon the simple shift (Caesar) cipher by using two mathematical keys instead of one. It is called “affine” because it uses an affine mathematical function."

The Formula for Encryption


 E(x) = (ax+b) mod 26


Where


• x = numerical value of plaintext letter


• a = multiplicative key


• b = additive key


• 26 = number of letters in the alphabet
